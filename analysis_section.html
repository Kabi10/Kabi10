Detailed Analysis: Agrimarket

Agrimarket is the crown jewel of the Android portfolio. At 156 MB and 258+ unit tests, it represents a production-grade agricultural marketplace. The offline-first Room architecture, trilingual support (Tamil/Sinhala/English), and OTP-based Sri Lankan phone auth make it impossible to fold into another app without massive refactoring. Verdict: definitive standalone mobile app.

Detailed Analysis: Moodlens

Moodlens is the largest repo by disk usage (157 MB), suggesting significant media assets or ML models. The mental health domain requires careful data handling and a focused UX. Verdict: standalone only. Never consolidate with commercial or social apps.

Final Strategic Recommendations

  1. Immediate Consolidation (Low Risk): Merge Hereticreader, Poet, Opssuite, Batchcard, Organiverse, Pathways, Crypto-Dashboard, ideas, Chembench, and Memory-Builder into Replit-Kabi as sub-artifacts or archive them. This removes 10 low-value repos.
  2. Monorepo Formation (Medium Risk): Create a "Kabi AI Lab" monorepo containing Replit-Kabi, Ai-Abstract-Art-Generator, Storyteller, Memoirstudio, Dreamweaver, and What-Ifs. Use pnpm workspaces and a shared Express backend.
  3. Developer OS Merge (Medium Risk): Merge kaiview and chorus into a single Python FastAPI monorepo. They share the same stack and user base (developers).
  4. Android Portfolio (No Consolidation): Keep Agrimarket, NurseShiftPay, Moodlens, diy-toolkit-rural, Snapassist, and android-ai-apps-suite as independent apps. They serve different users and have distinct backends.
  5. Archive Candidates: Attachment-List, Ai-Ghost-Writing, dream-weaver-ai, augment-code-best-practices, html-progression. These are superseded, empty, or low-value.
  6. Fork Maintenance: Keep all 12 forks active as open-source contribution portfolios. They demonstrate real-world collaboration skills.
