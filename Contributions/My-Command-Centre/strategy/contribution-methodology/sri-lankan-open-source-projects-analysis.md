## Executive Summary

This document provides a comprehensive analysis of open source projects originating from or relevant to Sri Lanka, with strategic assessment of contribution opportunities that align with social good objectives and professional development goals.

### Key Findings
- **11 major projects** identified across healthcare, governance, education, and infrastructure
- **High-impact opportunities** in healthcare systems (HMIS, OpenMRS, DHIS2)
- **Government digitization** projects with active development needs
- **Language technology** gaps requiring local expertise
- **Disaster management** systems with global relevance

### Strategic Priorities
1. **Healthcare Information Systems** - Primary focus area
2. **Government Digital Services** - Secondary focus
3. **Local Language Technology** - Specialized contribution area
4. **Disaster Management** - Global impact potential

## Project Portfolio Analysis

### Tier 1: Primary Contribution Targets

#### 1. HMIS (Health Management Information System)
**Repository:** `hmislk/hmis`  
**Status:** ✅ Active - Current primary contribution target  
**Strategic Value:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)

**Assessment:**
- Real-world deployment in Sri Lankan hospitals
- Direct impact on patient care and healthcare delivery
- Complex enterprise application providing learning opportunities
- Established contribution methodology and maintainer relationships

**Contribution Strategy:**
- Continue current focus on pharmacy module optimization
- Expand to billing system improvements
- Develop expertise in healthcare domain workflows
- Mentor new contributors to the project

#### 2. OpenMRS (Open Medical Record System)
**Repository:** `openmrs/openmrs-core`  
**Status:** 🔄 Evaluation phase  
**Strategic Value:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Maximum)

**Assessment:**
- Global platform with 1.6k stars, 3.9k forks
- Highly active community (14k+ commits)
- Modular architecture suitable for specialized contributions
- Potential for Sri Lankan healthcare system adoption

**Contribution Opportunities:**
- Develop Sinhala/Tamil localization modules
- Create Sri Lankan health data format plugins
- Build mobile companion apps for field health workers
- Contribute to disease surveillance modules

#### 3. DHIS2 (District Health Information Software)
**Repository:** `dhis2/dhis2-core`  
**Status:** 🔄 Evaluation phase  
**Strategic Value:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Assessment:**
- Used by Sri Lankan Ministry of Health
- Strong app platform for custom development
- 309 stars, 365 forks with active development
- Clear contribution guidelines and mentorship available

**Contribution Opportunities:**
- Develop Sinhala-language data entry applications
- Create ETL scripts for Sri Lankan health datasets
- Build visualization tools for local health metrics
- Contribute to GIS mapping components

### Tier 2: Secondary Contribution Targets

#### 4. Election Commission Integrated System (ECLK)
**Repository:** `ECLK/Nomination`  
**Status:** 🔄 Cyclical activity (election periods)  
**Strategic Value:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium-High)

**Assessment:**
- 19 stars, 33 forks with periodic high activity
- Critical for democratic processes in Sri Lanka
- Opportunities during election cycles
- Potential for transparency-enhancing side tools

**Contribution Strategy:**
- Monitor for pre-election development cycles
- Focus on documentation and deployment guides
- Develop voter education companion tools
- Create data visualization add-ons

#### 5. National Student Information System
**Repository:** `moe-lk/sis-php`  
**Status:** ⚠️ Maintenance needed  
**Strategic Value:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium-High)

**Assessment:**
- Covers 4.3 million students in 10,000+ schools
- 100+ open issues indicating maintenance needs
- PHP-based system requiring modernization
- Direct impact on education sector

**Contribution Opportunities:**
- Address data validation and UI issues
- Update dependencies and security patches
- Improve documentation for school IT staff
- Create mobile companion apps for teachers

#### 6. Medicines For LK
**Repository:** `LSFLK/MedicinesforLK`  
**Status:** 🔄 Post-crisis maintenance  
**Strategic Value:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium-High)

**Assessment:**
- 15 stars, 38 forks with TypeScript/Apache-2.0
- Humanitarian impact during 2022 crisis
- Potential for general healthcare supply chain use
- Modular architecture with separate APIs

**Contribution Strategy:**
- Generalize platform for ongoing hospital use
- Develop mobile donor applications
- Integrate with essential medicine databases
- Improve localization and user guides

## Strategic Contribution Framework

### Contribution Allocation Strategy

#### Primary Focus (60% of effort)
- **HMIS:** Continue current contributions (40%)
- **OpenMRS:** Begin evaluation and initial contributions (20%)

#### Secondary Focus (30% of effort)
- **DHIS2:** Explore app development opportunities (15%)
- **ECLK:** Monitor for election cycle opportunities (10%)
- **Medicines For LK:** Generalization and mobile development (5%)

#### Exploration & Learning (10% of effort)
- **Language Technology:** Specialized contributions
- **Sahana Eden:** Disaster management expertise
- **SIS:** Education sector impact assessment

### Contribution Types by Project Category

#### Healthcare Systems (HMIS, OpenMRS, DHIS2)
- **Core Development:** Bug fixes, performance improvements
- **Localization:** Sinhala/Tamil language support
- **Mobile Development:** Field worker applications
- **Data Integration:** Sri Lankan health data formats
- **Documentation:** Deployment and user guides

#### Government Digital Services (ECLK, SIS)
- **Maintenance:** Security updates, dependency management
- **Documentation:** Deployment and administration guides
- **Side Tools:** Transparency and education applications
- **Modernization:** Technology stack updates

#### Humanitarian & Social Impact (Medicines For LK, Sahana, MyHealth)
- **Platform Generalization:** Broader use case adaptation
- **Mobile Applications:** Accessibility improvements
- **Integration:** Cross-platform data sharing
- **Localization:** Multi-language support

## Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### High-Risk Factors
- **Project Abandonment:** Some projects may become inactive
- **Resource Constraints:** Limited time for multiple projects
- **Technical Complexity:** Healthcare domain learning curve
- **Community Dynamics:** Varying maintainer responsiveness

### Mitigation Strategies
- **Portfolio Diversification:** Maintain contributions across multiple projects
- **Community Engagement:** Build relationships with key maintainers
- **Domain Expertise:** Invest in healthcare technology education
- **Flexible Allocation:** Adjust effort based on project activity
